## Section 2: Pose Descriptions

### Tadasana

**FIELD**: introScript **CORRECTED**: Stand tall in Tadasana, the Mountain Pose. Root down through all four corners of your feet and engage your thighs. Lengthen your spine, relax your shoulders, and let your arms hang naturally with palms facing toward your body. **FIELD**: benefits **CORRECTED**: ✅ **REASON**: arms hang naturally with palms facing toward the body.

### Pranamasana

**FIELD**: introScript **CORRECTED**: Come into Pranamasana, the Prayer Pose. Stand with your feet together, bring your palms to meet at heart centre, and gently close your eyes or you can keep it open. Feel the balance and calm in this simple standing posture. **FIELD**: benefits **CORRECTED**: ✅ **REASON**: For beginners while standing and closing eyes, can lose balance.

### Hasta Uttanasana

**FIELD**: introScript **CORRECTED**: oScript **CORRECTED**: Lift into Hasta Uttanasana, the Raised Arms Pose. joint feet together and Inhale deeply as you sweep your arms overhead, arching gently backward. Keep your biceps beside your ears, open your chest and lift through upper back and find your balance. **FIELD**: benefits **CORRECTED**: ✅ **REASON**: need to open the chest and lifting arm should happen using upper back.

### Ashwa Sanchalanasana

Now place your both hands beside of your left foot at front and your left foot should be in same line with left knee. Look front. **FIELD**: benefits **CORRECTED**: ✅ **REASON**: By pressing dorsal, the upper part of your right foot on the mat, you can adjust not putting pressure on your right knee.

### Utthita Parsvakonasana

**FIELD**: introScript **CORRECTED**: Open into Utthita Parsvakonasana, the Extended Side Angle. Stand in Tadasana (Mountain Pose). Inhale and step or jump your feet 3.5 to 4 feet apart, extending your arms parallel to the floor. Turn your right foot out 90 degrees and the left foot slightly inward (about 45–60 degrees). Align the right heel with the center of the left arch. Exhale and bend your right knee so it is directly over your right ankle, aiming to bring the right thigh parallel to the floor. Place your right hand on the floor (outside your foot) or on a block. Alternatively, rest your right forearm on your right thigh for a lighter modification. Extend your left arm straight up and then over your left ear, creating a straight line from your left heel to your left fingertips. Turn your chest and ribs toward the ceiling. Look up toward your top hand, keeping the neck comfortable. Hold for 5-10 breaths. Inhale to come up, and repeat on the other side. **FIELD**: benefits **CORRECTED**: ✅ **REASON**: instruction not clear.

### Ardha Hanumanasana

**FIELD**: introScript **CORRECTED**: Ease into Ardha Hanumanasana, the Half Split. ## Section 6: Safety & Contraindications

### Ustrasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: Avoid if you have chronic back/neck injuries, high/low blood pressure, vertigo, migraines, or hernia.

### Setu Bandhasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: Avoid if you have high blood pressure, severe migraines, abdominal hernia, and pregnancy. Also should be avoided or modified if you have acute neck, shoulder, knee, or back injuries (such as a slipped disc or recent surgery).

### Adho Mukha Svanasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: Avoid if you have carpal tunnel syndrome, high blood pressure, headaches/migraines, detached retina, weak eye capillaries, heart conditions, or recent shoulder/wrist/back injuries.

### Sirsasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: not beginner pose.

### Virabhadrasana I

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: Avoid if you have balance difficulties or an injury to the hip, knees, back, or shoulders.

### Malasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: Should be avoided or modified if you have acute knee, ankle, or hip injuries, or severe lower back pain. Avoid if have recent surgeries in these areas, weak bone density, severe sciatica, or high blood pressure.

### Salabhasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**:  pregnancy, menstruation, or by individuals with severe back pain, slipped discs, hernia, abdominal tuberculosis, or peptic ulcers. Those with high blood pressure, vertigo, recent surgeries, or acute neck/hip injuries should also avoid this pose.

### Supta Matsyendrasana

**MISSING**: — **WARNING**: Should be avoided if you have recent surgeries (abdomen, hip, spine), severe back injuries, herniated discs, knee/shoulder injuries, or during pregnancy.
